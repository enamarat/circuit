An atom is the smallest unit of ordinary matter that forms a chemical element. It is composed of a nucleus and electrons rotating around the nucleus. The nucleus is made of one or more protons and a number of neutrons. More than 99.94% of an atom's mass is in the nucleus. The protons have a positive electric charge, the electrons have a negative electric charge, and the neutrons have no electric charge. If the number of protons and electrons are equal, then the atom is electrically neutral.
